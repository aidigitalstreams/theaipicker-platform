AIVA (Artificial Intelligence Virtual Artist) specialises in compositional music — orchestral, cinematic, ambient, and classical pieces. ## 4. Soundraw — Best for Creators

Soundraw takes a different approach — rather than generating from text prompts, users select genre, mood, instruments, and length, then the AI generates customisable tracks. Users can adjust individual elements (drums, melody, bass) after generation, providing more control than prompt-based tools. All tracks come with royalty-free licensing for commercial use across YouTube, podcasts, ads, and social media. The focus is on functional music for content creators rather than artistic music creation.

**Score: 73/100** | **Price:** $16.99/mo (Creator) / $29.99/mo (Artist)

[Try Soundraw →](https://soundraw.io) [AFFILIATE: soundraw]

## FAQ

**Is AI-generated music legal?**
The legal landscape is evolving rapidly. Suno's 2026 settlement with Warner Music established a commercial framework for AI music. Most tools offer commercial licensing on paid plans. However, copyright law around AI music remains unsettled globally — check each tool's licensing terms and consult legal advice for commercial use at scale.

**Can AI music be uploaded to Spotify?**
Yes. Boomy offers direct distribution to streaming platforms. Suno, Udio, and AIVA allow users to download tracks and distribute independently through aggregators like DistroKid or TuneCore.

**Which AI music tool has the best vocals?**
Suno V4 produces the most natural vocals — emotional expression, phrasing, and genre adaptation are best-in-class. Udio's vocals are also strong, with slightly cleaner mixing.
