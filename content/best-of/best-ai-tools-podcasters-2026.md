## 1. Descript — Best All-in-One Podcast Platform

Descript is the podcaster's Swiss army knife. Record, transcribe, edit (by editing the text transcript — delete a sentence and the audio is removed), add filler word removal, publish, and create social clips — all in one platform. The text-based editing is transformative for podcasters who dread traditional audio editing software. Studio Sound enhances audio quality post-recording, making decent microphone audio sound like it was recorded in a professional studio.

**What we liked:** Text-based audio editing is genuinely revolutionary for podcast workflows. Studio Sound cleans up audio impressively. The clip creation tool generates social-ready video clips automatically.

**What we didn't like:** The free tier is very limited (1 hour of transcription). The learning curve for advanced features is moderate. Large episode files can be slow to process.

**Pricing:** Free (1 hr/mo) / $24/mo (Hobbyist) / $33/mo (Pro)

**Score: 82/100**

## 3. Riverside — Best for Remote Recording

Riverside records each participant's audio and video locally at full quality — meaning internet connection issues don't degrade your recording. For interview-format podcasts where guests join remotely, this solves the biggest quality problem: Zoom-quality audio. The AI transcription is built in, and the platform generates social media clips from your recordings.

**What we liked:** Local recording eliminates internet-quality audio problems. The video recording is high quality for video podcasts. Built-in transcription and clip generation streamline post-production.

**What we didn't like:** The free tier is limited to 2 hours of recording. Solo recording (no guest) doesn't benefit from the local recording advantage. Audio-only podcasters are paying for video features they may not use.

**Pricing:** Free (2 hrs) / $19/mo (Standard) / $29/mo (Pro)

**Score: 76/100**

[Try Riverside Free →](https://riverside.fm) [AFFILIATE: riverside]

## 4. Otter.ai — Best Budget Transcription

Otter's 300 free minutes per month covers several podcast episodes — enough for most weekly shows. Speaker identification labels who said what, making the transcript useful for show notes and content repurposing. The searchable transcript archive means you can find any moment across your entire catalogue by keyword.

**What we liked:** 300 free minutes is the most generous transcription free tier. Speaker identification is reliable with 2-3 voices. The searchable archive grows more valuable over time.

**What we didn't like:** Accuracy drops with heavy accents, background noise, and crosstalk. No audio editing capabilities. The AI summary feature is basic compared to Descript's.

**Pricing:** Free (300 min/mo) / $16.99/mo (Pro)

**Score: 78/100**

## FAQ

**Can I make a podcast entirely with AI tools?**
You can automate most of the production workflow — recording, editing, transcription, show notes, clip creation, and promotion. The one thing AI can't replace is your unique perspective, personality, and expertise as a host. The best podcasts use AI to handle production so the host can focus on content.

**What's the minimum I need to spend?**
Zero. ChatGPT (free) for show notes, Otter (free, 300 min/month) for transcription, and Canva (free) for cover art covers the basics. The free tools handle a weekly podcast comfortably.

**Is Descript worth it for podcasters?**
If you edit your own episodes, yes — text-based editing is transformative. If you have a producer who handles editing, the value is lower. At $24-33/month, it replaces both your audio editor and transcription service.
