### 2. Grammarly — Best for Professional Communication

Every email, proposal, and social media post that leaves your business reflects your brand. Grammarly ($12/month) catches errors that spellcheck misses — awkward phrasing, tone mismatches, unclear sentences, and genuine grammatical mistakes. The 93-98% accuracy rate means almost every correction it suggests is right.

GrammarlyGO adds AI-powered writing assistance — rewrite paragraphs, adjust tone (professional, friendly, confident), and generate drafts from brief prompts. The browser extension works across Gmail, LinkedIn, social media, and any web form, so it protects your communication everywhere without changing your workflow.

**Why it matters for small businesses:** Poor writing costs credibility. A single embarrassing error in a client proposal can lose a deal. Grammarly is cheap insurance against that.

**Price:** Free (basic) / $12/mo (Premium)

[Try Grammarly Free →](https://grammarly.com) [AFFILIATE: grammarly]

**Why it matters for small businesses:** Professional design used to cost hundreds per project. Canva delivers 80% of the quality at 1% of the cost, with no design skills required.

## How to Prioritise Your AI Spending

Don't subscribe to all 10 tools at once. Start with the one that addresses your biggest time drain, prove the ROI, then add the next.

**If you're spending too much time writing:** Start with ChatGPT Plus ($20/month) or Grammarly ($12/month).

**If you're struggling to produce visual content:** Start with Canva (free).

**If meetings eat your day:** Start with Otter.ai (free — 300 min/month).

**If you need more website traffic:** Start with Surfer SEO ($79/month) or Perplexity for research (free).

The total cost of all 10 tools is approximately £200-250/month. But most small businesses need 3-4 of them, not all 10. Pick the ones that map to your actual bottlenecks.

## FAQ

**What's the single best AI tool for a small business?**
ChatGPT Plus ($20/month). It handles the widest range of tasks — writing, research, planning, analysis, code, spreadsheet formulas — and the ROI is immediate for any business owner who writes, plans, or researches as part of their day.

**Are free AI tools good enough for business use?**
For many tasks, yes. Canva's free tier covers basic design. Otter's free plan handles moderate meeting transcription. ChatGPT's free tier is usable for occasional tasks. The paid tiers add speed, volume, and advanced features — but the free tiers are legitimate starting points.

**Will AI tools replace employees?**
Not for most small businesses. AI tools replace tasks, not people. They handle the repetitive, time-consuming work — drafting emails, creating graphics, transcribing meetings — so your team (or you) can focus on the work that requires human judgment, relationships, and creativity.
