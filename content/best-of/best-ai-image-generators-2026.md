## 1. Midjourney — Best Overall (84/100)

Midjourney v7 remains the aesthetic ceiling for consumer AI image generation in 2026. Default output is publishable with minimal effort for illustration, editorial, concept art, and much photographic work. The Style Reference (--sref) and Character Reference (--cref) features enable the consistency-across-images use case that most creators actually need. The Pro app has closed the historical UX gap with web-native competitors.

**What we liked:** v7 default quality; Style Reference and Character Reference; strong parameter system; Personalise trains a genuine style multiplier; active creative community.

**What we didn't:** No API on consumer plans; weaker on literal prompt adherence than DALL-E 3; no custom model training; Discord history still leaks into the workflow.

**Pricing:** Basic $8/mo (annual), Standard $24/mo, Pro $48/mo, Mega $96/mo.

DALL-E 3 is the image generator embedded inside ChatGPT Plus. It wins on prompt adherence — for complex prompts with many specified elements, DALL-E 3 follows the brief more literally than Midjourney or Leonardo. It also wins on accessibility: most users pay for ChatGPT anyway, and image generation comes "free" with the subscription.

**What we liked:** Best-in-class prompt adherence; conversational refinement inside ChatGPT; text rendering inside images reasonable; bundled via ChatGPT Plus at $20/month.

**What we didn't:** No dedicated editor or Canvas; no image-to-image for general users; no custom model training; generation volume throttled under ChatGPT Plus; no standalone API for most users.

**Pricing:** Available via ChatGPT Plus ($20/mo), ChatGPT Team ($25/user/mo), and Enterprise.

## How to Choose the Right AI Image Generator

**Single-image aesthetic quality →** Midjourney. For hero images, editorial work, illustration, concept art, and anything where "the best one image" matters most.

**Prompt adherence or you already pay for ChatGPT →** DALL-E 3. For complex briefs where literal prompt following matters, or when the image is a one-off ideation alongside a conversation.

**Iterative workflows, series work, custom training →** Leonardo. For brand consistency, game assets, product variants, and anything where producing 20 images of the same style matters more than one perfect hero shot.

**Text inside images (signs, logos, typography) →** Ideogram (review coming). Narrower tool than the three above but best-in-class for the specific job.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the best AI image generator in 2026?**
Midjourney (84/100) for aesthetic ceiling. DALL-E 3 (82/100) for prompt adherence and ChatGPT integration. Leonardo AI (81/100) for workflow depth and value. The right answer depends on the use case.

**Is Midjourney better than DALL-E 3?**
For default image quality and creative aesthetics, Midjourney leads. For literal prompt adherence and conversational image generation inside ChatGPT, DALL-E 3 leads. Most creators serious about image generation end up subscribing to at least one of them.

**Can I use these AI images commercially?**
All three tools include commercial licensing on their paid plans. Free/trial tiers often restrict commercial use. Always verify current licensing on each provider's official site before publishing.

**What is the cheapest AI image generator?**
Leonardo AI has a free tier with 150 daily tokens — the most usable free tier among the three. For paid plans, Midjourney Basic at $8/month (annual) is the lowest sticker price with regular access.

**Does any of these tools support custom model training?**
Leonardo AI supports custom model training on Artisan tier ($24/month annual) and above, plus Elements (LoRA-style modifiers) across paid plans. Midjourney and DALL-E 3 do not currently offer custom model training to general users.
