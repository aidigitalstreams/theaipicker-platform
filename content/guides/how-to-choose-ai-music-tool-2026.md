## The Short Answer

If you want complete songs with vocals from a text prompt, **Suno** is the most capable option — it generates lyrics, vocals, and full instrumentation. If audio production quality matters most, **Udio** produces the cleanest, most professional-sounding output. If you need instrumental music for video, games, or film, **AIVA** specialises in orchestral and cinematic composition. This guide helps you identify which use case matches yours.

## What AI Music Tools Actually Do

AI music generation tools create original music from user input — typically a text prompt describing the genre, mood, instrumentation, and style you want. The most advanced tools (Suno, Udio) generate complete songs including vocals and lyrics. Others (AIVA, Soundraw) focus on instrumental tracks. The simplest (Boomy) generate basic music with one-click interfaces.

The category has evolved rapidly. In early 2025, AI-generated music sounded noticeably artificial. By 2026, the best tools produce output that is increasingly difficult to distinguish from human-made music in casual listening. The legal landscape has also shifted — Suno's settlement with Warner Music in early 2026 established a commercial framework that didn't exist a year ago.

## The Five Questions That Decide Your Choice

### 1. Do You Need Vocals or Just Instrumentals?

This is the biggest fork in the category.

**Full songs with vocals:** Suno and Udio are the only serious options. Both generate complete songs — lyrics, vocals, melody, and instrumentation — from text prompts. Suno's V4 model produces the most natural vocal delivery, with emotional expression and phrasing that sounds genuinely human across genres. Udio's vocals are slightly cleaner in mixing but less emotionally expressive.

**Instrumental music only:** AIVA and Soundraw are purpose-built for this. AIVA specialises in compositional music — orchestral, cinematic, ambient, and classical pieces with deep understanding of musical theory. Soundraw focuses on functional background music for content creators, with adjustable elements (drums, melody, bass) after generation.

**Both:** If you need both vocal tracks and instrumentals, Suno or Udio handle both, though their instrumental output is less refined than AIVA's for orchestral and cinematic work.

### 2. What Are You Using the Music For?

**YouTube videos, podcasts, social media:** Soundraw is specifically designed for this. All tracks come with royalty-free licensing for commercial content creation. The customisation controls let you match track length to your video, adjust energy levels for different sections, and tweak instruments to fit your brand.

**Film scoring, game soundtracks, cinematic content:** AIVA is the strongest choice. Its orchestral compositions have the depth and dynamics that cinematic work requires. The Pro plan (€49/month) includes full copyright ownership, which matters for commercial releases.

**Music creation and artistic expression:** Suno and Udio are where creative experimentation happens. Describe a genre mashup, specify a mood, give it lyrics — and hear what comes back. The creative range is extraordinary, covering everything from lo-fi hip-hop to death metal to bossa nova.

**Streaming platform distribution:** Boomy is the only tool with built-in distribution to Spotify, Apple Music, and other streaming platforms. However, the music quality is significantly lower than Suno or Udio. If you want to distribute AI-generated music to streaming platforms with better quality, use Suno or Udio and distribute independently through DistroKid or TuneCore.

### 3. How Much Control Do You Want?

**Minimal control (just give me music):** Boomy is the simplest — choose a genre, click create, get a song. No prompt engineering, no musical knowledge needed. The tradeoff is that output quality is basic.

**Text prompt control:** Suno and Udio work from natural language descriptions. "An upbeat indie folk song about morning coffee with acoustic guitar and light percussion" produces surprisingly accurate results. The more specific your prompt, the closer the output matches your vision.

**Detailed element control:** Soundraw gives you post-generation control over individual elements — adjust the drums independently from the melody, change energy levels section by section, extend or shorten specific parts. This is the closest to "mixing" that any AI tool offers.

**Compositional control:** AIVA allows you to set key, tempo, duration, and instrumentation before generation. You can also edit the generated MIDI output in a piano roll editor. This is the most technically detailed control in the category, but it requires some musical knowledge to use effectively.

### 4. What About Copyright and Licensing?

The legal landscape around AI-generated music is evolving, and licensing terms vary significantly between tools.

**Suno:** Free plan output is non-commercial. Pro ($10/month) and Premier ($30/month) plans include commercial licensing. The Warner Music settlement established a precedent for commercial AI music, but the broader legal picture remains unsettled.

**Udio:** Similar structure to Suno — free tier is non-commercial, paid plans include commercial rights.

**AIVA:** The free plan retains copyright for AIVA. The Standard plan (€15/month) grants a limited licence. Only the Pro plan (€49/month) grants full copyright ownership to the user.

**Soundraw:** All paid plans include royalty-free commercial licensing for content creation (YouTube, podcasts, ads, social media). This is the simplest, most permissive licensing model in the category.

**Boomy:** Paid plans allow distribution to streaming platforms, with Boomy taking a share of streaming revenue. The licensing terms are simpler but the revenue share model means you never fully own the commercial rights.

### 5. What's Your Budget?

AI music generation is one of the most affordable AI categories.

**Free options:** Suno offers 50 credits daily (enough for several complete songs). Udio has a limited free tier. AIVA allows 3 downloads per month free (AIVA retains copyright). Boomy has a limited free tier.

**Under £15/month:** Suno Pro ($10/month), Udio Standard ($10/month), Decktopus ($9.99/month), and AIVA Standard (€15/month) all fall here. For most users, Suno Pro offers the best value — unlimited non-commercial generations and commercial licensing for released tracks.

**Above £15/month:** Soundraw ($16.99/month) and AIVA Pro (€49/month) are the premium options. Soundraw's price is justified by the unlimited royalty-free licensing. AIVA Pro is only necessary if you need full copyright ownership for cinematic or commercial releases.

## What to Avoid

**Don't assume AI music is legally risk-free.** While Suno's Warner settlement was a landmark, copyright law around AI-generated music remains unsettled globally. If you're using AI music commercially at scale, check each tool's current licensing terms and consider legal advice.

**Don't judge quality from one generation.** AI music tools produce variable results. Generate 5-10 tracks from the same prompt and pick the best. The variation between outputs can be dramatic — from impressive to unusable from the same input.

**Don't use Boomy and expect Suno quality.** The simplest tools produce the lowest-quality output. If audio quality matters, invest the small learning curve in Suno or Udio rather than settling for Boomy's one-click approach.

## FAQ

**Can I upload AI-generated music to Spotify?**
Yes. Boomy offers direct distribution to streaming platforms. Suno, Udio, and AIVA allow you to download tracks and distribute independently through aggregators like DistroKid or TuneCore. Each platform has its own policies on AI content disclosure — check current requirements before uploading.

**Will AI music tools replace musicians?**
Not in 2026. AI tools excel at generating functional music — background tracks, content filler, quick prototypes. For live performance, artistic expression, and music that requires genuine emotional depth, human musicians remain irreplaceable. The tools are best understood as a new instrument, not a replacement for musicians.

**Which AI music tool has the best vocals?**
Suno V4 produces the most natural, emotionally expressive vocals across genres. Udio's vocals are technically cleaner in terms of mixing but slightly less natural in delivery.
