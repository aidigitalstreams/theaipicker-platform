## What Is Writesonic?

Writesonic is an AI-powered content platform that combines long-form article writing, short-form copy generation, an AI chat assistant (Chatsonic), and built-in SEO tools into a single subscription. Launched in 2021, it's grown into one of the most feature-dense AI writing tools on the market.

The platform's headline feature is **Article Writer 6.0** — a long-form content engine that pulls real-time data from the web, analyses competitor content, and structures articles around search intent. It can produce articles up to 5,000 words, complete with source-backed claims and internal linking suggestions. For content teams focused on organic traffic, this is genuinely useful.

In 2026, Writesonic added **Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)** — a tool that tracks how your brand appears in AI-generated answers across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and other AI platforms. It's one of the few AI writing tools looking beyond traditional SEO into how content performs in AI search results.

The platform runs on multiple AI models including GPT-4o, Claude 3.7 Sonnet, Gemini, Grok, and Perplexity, automatically selecting the best model for each task.

## Key Features

### Article Writer 6.0

The core of Writesonic's appeal. This long-form content generator pulls live data from the web to produce factually current articles — something most competitors can't match without plugins. It analyses top-ranking competitors for your target keyword and structures content to match search intent.

Based on user reviews, accuracy sits around 85% for mainstream topics. That's solid for a first draft, but it means roughly 1 in 6 factual claims may need checking — particularly on niche or technical subjects. The output is SEO-structured by default, which saves time compared to writing in a generic tool and then optimising separately.

### Chatsonic

Writesonic's conversational AI assistant with real-time web browsing, file uploads (PDFs, images, audio), and access to multiple AI models. It's more capable than a basic chatbot — users can research topics, fact-check claims, analyse documents, and generate images within the same interface.

Community feedback rates Chatsonic highly for research-heavy workflows. The web browsing feature means conversations reference current information rather than relying solely on training data, which is a practical advantage over Jasper Chat.

### Built-In SEO Tools

This is Writesonic's biggest competitive advantage. The platform includes keyword research, content optimisation scoring, competitor analysis, content gap analysis, and meta description generation — all built into the same workflow. No separate subscription required.

For context: Jasper users typically add Surfer SEO ($89/month) for comparable SEO functionality. That pushes Jasper's effective cost to $128-158/month for a similar SEO-ready writing stack. Writesonic includes it all in the base price.

### GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)

A genuinely innovative feature that tracks your brand's visibility across AI search engines. When someone asks ChatGPT or Perplexity "what's the best AI writing tool?" — GEO shows whether your brand gets mentioned, how often, and with what sentiment. It's forward-looking and unique in the market — no other AI writing tool offers this yet.

Available on the Professional plan ($249/month), so it's not cheap — but for teams serious about AI-era visibility, it's a differentiator.

### Brand Voice Training

Users can train Writesonic to write in their specific style by uploading documents, adding website links, or pasting text samples. The system learns your voice over time. It's functional and gets the job done, though our research suggests Jasper's brand voice system is more refined for large-scale marketing operations.

### Plagiarism Checker

Built-in plagiarism detection — a feature that both Jasper includes and Copy.ai lacks. It's a practical inclusion that saves the cost and friction of a separate plagiarism tool.

## What We Didn't Like

**The credit system is confusing.** Unlike Copy.ai's simple "unlimited words" approach, Writesonic uses a credit-based system that varies by plan and task type. Multiple user reviews flag difficulty tracking remaining usage. This creates anxiety about running out mid-project and makes cost-per-article calculations harder.

**Billing concerns are a red flag.** Multiple Trustpilot reviews mention unexpected charges after cancellation. While this may not reflect the typical experience, it's been flagged enough times to warrant caution. We'd recommend carefully reviewing cancellation terms before subscribing.

**Content accuracy requires checking.** The 85% accuracy rate for mainstream topics means roughly 1 in 6 factual claims may need verification. On niche or technical subjects, the error rate is higher. Every article needs a fact-checking pass before publishing — which is true of all AI writers, but worth emphasising.

**The price-to-value ratio lags behind Copy.ai.** At $39/month (annual) for 15 articles with a credit limit, Writesonic is more expensive per word than Copy.ai's $24/month for unlimited words. The SEO tools justify the premium for search-focused users, but for general content creation, Copy.ai offers better raw value.

### Is Writesonic free?

There's a free plan with 10,000 words per month, but it's limited to GPT-3.5 and basic templates. The output quality is noticeably lower than the paid plans. It's useful for evaluating the interface and basic workflow, but for serious content production you'll need the Lite plan ($39/month annual) or above.

### Is Writesonic better than Jasper?

For brand-consistent marketing content, Jasper is still stronger. See our full [Writesonic vs Jasper comparison](/writesonic-vs-jasper/).

### Is Writesonic better than Copy.ai?
