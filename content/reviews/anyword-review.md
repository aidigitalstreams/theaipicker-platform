## What Is Anyword?

Anyword is a generative AI writing platform purpose-built for performance marketers. Unlike general-purpose AI writers that optimise for readability or SEO, Anyword optimises for **conversion**. Its platform is trained on billions of marketing data points and generates a Predictive Performance Score (0-100) for every piece of copy, forecasting how likely it is to outperform alternatives before you ever publish.

The company claims over 1 million marketers use the platform, with named customers including Amazon, Greenhouse, and IBM. Unlike Jasper or Copy.ai, which position themselves as broad content tools, Anyword's pitch is narrow and specific: write copy that converts.

The 2026 platform supports ad copy (Meta, Google, LinkedIn), email marketing, landing pages, social posts, blog articles, and SMS — all through over 100 marketing templates. Its differentiator is the data layer underneath: every generation is scored, and users can train the AI on their own past campaign performance to get increasingly accurate predictions over time.

## Key Features

### Predictive Performance Score

The core feature. Anyword evaluates every piece of generated copy against its database of similar content and returns a score from 0 to 100 predicting how it will perform. According to Anyword's own published figures, the model achieves 82% accuracy when predicting which of two content variations will convert better.

For performance marketers running paid campaigns, this is genuinely useful. Instead of A/B testing a dozen headlines in-market and burning ad spend, you can filter for high-score variations before launching. Our research found this feature is frequently cited in user reviews as the reason marketers stick with Anyword over cheaper alternatives.

The catch: 82% accuracy means 1 in 5 predictions are wrong, so this is a directional tool, not a crystal ball. Real-world testing still matters.

### Custom Brand Voice

Anyword lets users train a custom AI model on their own marketing data — past ads, landing pages, email campaigns — so generated content reflects the brand's actual voice and tone rather than generic AI output. This is available on the Data-Driven plan and above.

User feedback suggests this is one of the stronger brand voice implementations in the category, rivalling Jasper's. The difference: Anyword's brand voice is trained on *performance data* (what has worked), not just stylistic samples (how you sound). For conversion-focused teams, this distinction matters.

### 100+ Marketing Templates

Anyword ships with over 100 templates covering every major paid and organic channel: Facebook ads, Google Ads, LinkedIn, landing page headlines, email subject lines, SMS, product descriptions, and more. Each template is optimised for the channel's format constraints and audience behaviour.

This is deeper than Copy.ai or Jasper for paid-media-specific workflows. Where general tools give you a "blog intro" template, Anyword gives you "LinkedIn Sponsored Content headline, 150-character limit, B2B SaaS audience."

### Audience Targeting & Segmentation

Users can define target audiences (by demographics, interests, behaviour) and Anyword tailors copy to resonate with each segment. This pairs with the Predictive Score — the platform predicts performance *for that specific audience*, not in the abstract.

For multi-audience campaigns, this is a real workflow advantage. For solo creators writing general content, it's overkill.

### Team Collaboration

Multiple users can work on the same project simultaneously, with shared brand voices, approved templates, and campaign histories. This is standard in the paid tiers and competitive with Jasper's collaboration features.

### SEO Tools

Anyword includes keyword targeting and SEO scoring, though it's lighter than Writesonic's dedicated SEO toolkit. For teams whose primary channel is paid, this is enough. For SEO-first teams, Writesonic is a better pick.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Anyword better than Jasper?

For paid marketing copy, yes — Anyword's Predictive Performance Score is something Jasper doesn't offer. For long-form content and brand-consistent marketing writing, Jasper is stronger. The right choice depends on whether conversion forecasting or long-form polish matters more to your team.

### Is the Predictive Performance Score accurate?

Anyword claims 82% accuracy in predicting which of two variations will convert better. That's a strong figure, but it means roughly 1 in 5 predictions are wrong. Treat it as a directional signal, not a guaranteed outcome. Real-world testing still matters.

### Does Anyword have a free plan?

No — Anyword offers a free trial, but not a permanent free tier. This is different from Writesonic (10,000 words/mo forever) and Copy.ai (2,000 words/mo forever). If you want to evaluate without a time limit, those alternatives are better starting points.

### Is Anyword worth $79/month?

For performance marketers running paid campaigns, yes — if you're using the Predictive Score on real ad spend decisions, it pays back quickly. For general content creators, no — cheaper tools like Copy.ai or Writesonic offer better value.

### What makes Anyword different from ChatGPT?

ChatGPT generates copy but gives you no data about how it will perform. Anyword generates copy *and* forecasts its conversion likelihood against a database of real marketing performance data. For paid campaigns, that data layer is the entire value proposition.
