## What Is ChatGPT?

ChatGPT is OpenAI's consumer-facing chatbot product, launched in November 2022 and now the most widely used AI assistant globally. The product sits on top of OpenAI's frontier models (GPT-5 as the flagship at time of writing; GPT-4o for multimodal work; o-series reasoning models for deeper thinking tasks), wrapping them in a UI designed for chat, document work, voice conversation, image generation, and increasingly video generation via Sora.

ChatGPT's moat is breadth. No other chatbot matches the range of integrated capabilities — voice, image, video generation, custom GPTs, Canvas document editing, web browsing, code execution, file analysis — all in one subscription. Power users consistently pay for ChatGPT *and* one other chatbot (usually Claude or Perplexity) because ChatGPT covers more ground than any alternative does on its own.

## Key Features

**Underlying models.** GPT-5 is the flagship; GPT-4o handles multimodal tasks (voice, image analysis); o-series (o3, o3-mini, o4) handle reasoning-heavy tasks where the model thinks before answering. Plus subscribers access the full suite with usage caps; Pro subscribers get higher limits and priority access.

**Advanced Voice Mode.** Real-time, full-duplex voice conversation with emotional range, interruption handling, and video input (the model can see through a phone camera during voice calls). In 2026 this remains the category-leading consumer voice implementation.

**GPTs and the GPT Store.** Users create custom assistants with tailored instructions, uploaded knowledge, and tool integrations. The GPT Store surfaces the best community-built GPTs. This is the feature that turned ChatGPT from a chatbot into a platform.

**Canvas.** A document-and-code editing surface that runs alongside the chat — the user and the model can edit the same document collaboratively, track versions, and iterate on structure. Strong for long-form writing and code review.

**Image generation (DALL-E 3) and video generation (Sora).** Plus and above generate images without extra cost. Sora video generation is available to Plus ($20/mo) with significant limits and to Pro ($200/mo) with higher allowances.

**Projects.** Folder-style organisation for grouping related conversations and shared files — similar to Claude Projects but with looser integration.

**Web browsing, code execution, file upload.** All included on paid tiers; free tier has throttled access.

## What We Didn't Like

- Pro tier at $200/month is a steep step up from Plus. Many users who max out Plus do not need everything Pro offers.
- Usage caps on Plus are occasionally frustrating for power users (especially for o3 reasoning and Sora).
- Output quality on long-form creative and analytical writing is competitive but not category-leading — Claude is the stronger pick for heavy writing workflows, based on consistent user feedback across practitioner communities.
- Feature sprawl means some subfeatures feel half-finished compared to specialist tools (Sora is a reasonable video generator but Runway is the better dedicated tool).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is ChatGPT still the best AI chatbot in 2026?**
It is the best default pick for broad capability. For heavy writing or reasoning, Claude is a strong alternative. For research, Perplexity is worth trialling. Many users pay for ChatGPT plus one other.

**Is ChatGPT Plus worth $20/month?**
For regular users (more than 10-20 meaningful conversations per week), yes. The capability step from free to Plus is significant — faster models, reliable access, voice mode, Canvas, GPTs.

**Is ChatGPT Pro worth $200/month?**
Only if you are regularly hitting Plus usage caps on o-series reasoning or Sora. For most users, Plus is enough.

**Does ChatGPT train on my conversations?**
The free and Plus tiers train on conversations by default; you can opt out in settings. Team and Enterprise tiers do not train on your data by default.

**What is the difference between GPT-5 and o3?**
GPT-5 is the general-purpose flagship. o3 is a reasoning-specialised model that thinks step by step before answering — slower but more accurate on hard logical, mathematical, and coding problems.
