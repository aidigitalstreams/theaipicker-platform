## What Is Suno?

Suno is a full-song AI music generator that creates complete tracks — lyrics, vocals, instrumentals, and arrangement — from text prompts. Unlike royalty-free music libraries (Epidemic Sound, Artlist) that offer pre-made tracks, and unlike production tools (AIVA, Soundraw) that generate instrumental backgrounds, Suno generates original songs with AI-performed vocals that sound remarkably human.

The platform has evolved rapidly: v4.5 (May 2025) extended songs to 8 minutes, v5 improved audio quality and prompt adherence, and v5.5 (March 2026) added voice cloning, custom model training, and personalised taste learning. Each version has represented a meaningful quality leap.

## Key Features

**Full-song generation.** Describe what you want ("upbeat indie rock song about Monday mornings, female vocals, 120 BPM") and Suno generates a complete track — lyrics, melody, vocals, instruments, arrangement. The speed is impressive: a full song in under a minute.

**Vocal generation.** AI-generated singing vocals from lyrics. This is Suno's headline feature and the one that draws the most attention. Quality varies — some outputs are eerily convincing, others have an uncanny quality that listeners notice. v5.5 added voice cloning (using your own voice) for more consistent vocal performance.

**Genre range.** 1,200+ style options spanning pop, rock, hip-hop, electronic, classical, jazz, country, R&B, and experimental genres. Style accuracy varies — mainstream genres produce more convincing results than niche or complex styles.

**Song length.** Up to 8 minutes per track on v4.5 and later. Earlier versions were limited to 2-4 minutes.

**Stem separation.** Studio mode splits generated tracks into 12 individual stems: vocals, drums, bass, keys, synths, strings, brass, woodwinds, percussion, effects, and other. This is useful for remixing and production work.

**Custom styles and personas.** Reusable style presets with granular control over instruments, tempo, mood, and voice type. v5.5 added custom model fine-tuning and personalised AI taste learning.

## What We Didn't Like

Vocal quality is the persistent weakness. Despite improvements with each version, community consensus is that most AI vocal outputs still sound "off" to attentive listeners. This limits Suno's usefulness for anything where vocal quality is critical (released music, professional video soundtracks, client work).

The legal landscape is a genuine concern. Active copyright lawsuits from UMG, Sony, GEMA, and independent artists create uncertainty about the platform's future terms and the legal safety of using generated music commercially. Suno's paid plans include commercial rights, but the company does not indemnify users against copyright claims — you bear the legal risk if generated music sounds like copyrighted work.

Platform stability is uncertain. The Warner deal (November 2025) changed terms mid-stream — adding download caps and ending free-tier downloads. Fast-moving startups in active litigation can change terms again. Users who build workflows around Suno should have contingency plans.

## FAQ

**Is Suno free?**
Yes — the free plan includes 50 credits per day (~10 songs) using the v4.5-All model. Free-tier songs cannot be used commercially.

**Can I use Suno music commercially?**
Pro ($10/mo) and Premier ($30/mo) plans include commercial rights for generated music. However, Suno does not indemnify users against copyright claims — the legal risk sits with the user.

**Is Suno legal?**
Suno is a legal platform, but it faces active copyright lawsuits from UMG, Sony, GEMA (Germany), and independent artists over its training data. The outcome of these cases may affect future terms. Warner Music settled in November 2025 and partnered with Suno on licensed models.

**How good are Suno vocals?**
Vocal quality has improved significantly with each model version but remains inconsistent. Research and community feedback suggest roughly 75% of vocal outputs still sound noticeably AI-generated to attentive listeners. Instrumentals are generally stronger.

**How does Suno compare to Udio?**
Both generate full songs with vocals from text prompts. Both face copyright litigation. Community opinion is split on which produces better results — the quality is close enough that personal preference matters more than objective differences.
