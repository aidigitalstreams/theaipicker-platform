## What Is Recraft?

Recraft is an AI-powered design tool built specifically for professional designers, brand managers, and creative teams. Unlike general-purpose image generators that output pixel-based images from text prompts, Recraft generates both raster images and vector (SVG) graphics, renders readable text within images, and learns brand styles from uploaded references — capabilities that address the actual needs of design workflows rather than treating image generation as a standalone novelty.

The platform operates on an infinite canvas interface similar to Figma, where users generate, arrange, edit, and iterate on images in a spatial workspace. This design-first approach means Recraft competes less with Midjourney's artistic exploration and more with the intersection of Adobe Illustrator and AI generation.

## Key Features

**Vector SVG Generation.** Recraft's standout feature. Generate clean, scalable vector graphics — logos, icons, illustrations, UI elements — directly from text prompts. The vectors are production-ready SVGs that can be imported into Figma, Illustrator, or any design tool. No other AI image generator offers native vector output at this quality.

**Text Rendering.** Reliable text within generated images — mockups, social media graphics, ad creatives, packaging designs — where the text is legible and properly formatted. This has historically been the weakest point of AI image generators; Recraft handles it better than any competitor we've researched.

**Brand Consistency.** Upload reference images representing your brand's visual identity, define custom colour palettes, and set style presets. The AI learns your style and applies it consistently across subsequent generations. This transforms Recraft from a one-off generation tool into a brand asset production system.

**Infinite Canvas.** A spatial workspace familiar to Figma and Miro users. Generate images directly on the canvas, arrange them, compare variations side-by-side, and build cohesive asset sets. This is fundamentally different from the "generate, download, start again" workflow of most AI image tools.

**Style Presets.** Extensive built-in styles — realistic, illustration, flat design, 3D render, watercolour, pixel art, and more. Custom styles can be created from uploaded references, allowing users to define and replicate specific aesthetic directions.

**Inpainting & Outpainting.** Edit regions of generated images by describing changes (inpainting) or extend images beyond their original boundaries (outpainting). Both work well for iterative design refinement.

## What We Didn't Like

**Smaller ecosystem.** Midjourney has a massive community, extensive tutorials, and a rich Discord ecosystem. Recraft's community is growing but significantly smaller, meaning fewer shared prompts, tips, and workflow examples.

**Design-focused positioning limits appeal.** If you want dreamlike artistic imagery, abstract visual exploration, or purely creative generation, Midjourney remains more versatile. Recraft is optimised for design work — which is its strength but also its boundary.

**Custom model training not available.** Recraft offers brand references and custom styles but doesn't allow fine-tuning on your own images (LoRA-style training). For users who need this, Stable Diffusion remains the option.
