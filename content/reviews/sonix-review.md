## What Is Sonix?

Sonix is an AI-powered transcription platform that converts audio and video files into searchable, editable text. What sets it apart from API-first competitors like Rev is the emphasis on the post-transcription workflow: Sonix's in-browser editor syncs audio playback to transcript text word-by-word, making corrections fast and intuitive.

Sonix supports 53+ languages, integrates with video editing tools (Adobe Premiere, Final Cut Pro), video conferencing platforms (Zoom, Teams, Google Meet), and offers AI-powered analysis features including summarisation, sentiment analysis, and topic detection.

## Key Features

**In-browser editor.** The standout feature. Audio playback syncs to the transcript word-by-word — click any word and playback jumps to that moment. This makes correction work significantly faster than competitors that treat the transcript as a static text document.

**AI analysis.** Premium subscribers can access AI-powered summaries, sentiment analysis, topic detection, and custom prompt analysis on their transcripts. This transforms transcripts from raw text into structured data.

**Language support.** 53+ languages for both transcription and translation. Cross-language workflows (transcribe in Spanish, translate to English) are supported natively.

**Integrations.** Native connections to Zoom, Microsoft Teams, Google Meet, Webex, Adobe Premiere, Final Cut Pro, and Zapier. The video production integrations are particularly strong — Sonix exports subtitle tracks directly in the formats editors need.

**Speaker diarisation.** Automatic speaker identification with timestamps across all plans.

**Export formats.** 20+ export formats including TXT, DOCX, SRT, VTT, PDF — covering both document and subtitle use cases.

## What We Liked

The editing experience is the clear winner. Sonix understood that transcription is not finished when the AI outputs text — the real work is correction and formatting. The synced editor, combined with keyboard shortcuts for playback control, makes correction work 2-3x faster than editing a raw text document alongside a separate audio player.

The integration depth with video production tools is also strong. Exporting subtitle tracks in SRT/VTT format directly to Premiere or Final Cut saves a step that every video producer knows is tedious.

## What We Didn't Like

Accuracy on conversational audio is inconsistent. If your content features casual speech, multiple speakers talking over each other, or non-native English accents, expect to spend meaningful time on corrections. This is a category-wide limitation, but Sonix doesn't overcome it as well as its marketing suggests.

The layered pricing model is confusing. Paying both a monthly fee and per-hour charges requires more mental effort to budget than a simple subscription with included hours. The 30-minute free trial is also short — just enough to test the interface, not enough to evaluate accuracy on your specific content type.
