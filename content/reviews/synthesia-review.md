## What Is Synthesia?

Synthesia is an AI video platform that generates avatar-led videos from text scripts. Users paste a script, choose an avatar and voice, and Synthesia produces a finished video of that avatar speaking the script with synchronised lip movements and natural delivery.

Founded in 2017 and headquartered in London, Synthesia is one of the longest-established avatar platforms in the market and has built its reputation on enterprise sales — over half of the Fortune 100 reportedly use Synthesia for internal training and corporate communications. The 2026 platform offers 230+ stock avatars, 140+ languages, custom avatar creation (on enterprise tiers), and a structured editor designed around teams producing video at scale.

Synthesia competes directly with HeyGen on the high-realism end and Colossyan on the team-collaboration end. Its pitch is unmistakable: the safest, most predictable AI avatar tool for organisations that need to ship video at scale without surprises.

## Key Features

### 230+ Stock Avatars

Synthesia's stock avatar library is one of the largest in the category, covering varied demographics, professional contexts, and styles. The avatars are designed for corporate, educational, and training video — the bias is towards "presenter-style" delivery rather than the more expressive social-content energy you'll see in some HeyGen stock avatars. For most enterprise use cases this is the right choice.

### 140+ Languages and Accents

Synthesia supports 140+ languages and regional accents — narrower than HeyGen's 175+ but still best-in-class for enterprise needs. Voice and avatar can be mixed across language boundaries, and the platform handles localisation workflows particularly well for teams producing the same content across multiple regions.

### Custom Avatars (Enterprise Tier)

Synthesia offers custom avatar creation, but it's gated behind enterprise contracts rather than offered at a self-serve price like HeyGen's $99/mo tier. The trade-off is that Synthesia's custom avatar process is more controlled — typically involving a managed onboarding session and a higher-fidelity capture process. For enterprises wanting brand-exact custom avatars with strict consent and usage governance, this is the right model. For solo creators or small teams who want a custom avatar today at a self-serve price point, HeyGen is the better fit.

### Compliance Stack — SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, GDPR

This is the single biggest reason Synthesia wins enterprise procurement battles. SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, and GDPR compliance are not just badges — they're the baseline requirements for getting an AI tool past most enterprise security teams. Synthesia also offers content moderation controls and avatar consent tracking that are genuinely more mature than competitors. For regulated industries (financial services, healthcare, government, pharma), this stack alone often makes the buying decision.

### PowerPoint Integration

Native import from PowerPoint converts existing slide decks directly into avatar-led videos. For corporate L&D and internal comms teams sitting on hundreds of training decks, this is a significant productivity unlock. HeyGen has similar integrations but Synthesia's PowerPoint pipeline is more mature.

### Team Collaboration

Multi-user workflows, role-based permissions, brand kit management, and review/approval flows are all built in. This is video production for organisations, not for solo creators — and it shows in the structure.

### Predictable Pricing — Minutes per Month

Synthesia bills by video minutes per month rather than credits. The $18/mo Starter tier includes 10 minutes; Creator at $64/mo includes 30 minutes; enterprise tiers include custom allocations. This model is much easier to forecast and budget than HeyGen's credit system, which trades flexibility for predictability.

## What We Didn't Like

**Language coverage trails HeyGen.** 140+ vs 175+ matters less than you'd think (most teams need a handful of languages, not all of them) but if you genuinely need maximum coverage, HeyGen wins this metric.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Synthesia better than HeyGen?

Depends on what you're optimising for. HeyGen wins on avatar realism (Avatar IV), language coverage (175+ vs 140+), and accessible self-serve custom avatars ($99/mo vs enterprise-only). Synthesia wins on enterprise compliance (SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, GDPR), pricing predictability (minutes vs credits), team collaboration features, and PowerPoint integration. Our [Synthesia vs HeyGen comparison](/synthesia-vs-heygen/) covers this in detail.

### How much does Synthesia cost?

Synthesia's paid plans start at $18/mo (Starter, 10 video minutes per month, annual billing). Creator is $64/mo for 30 minutes per month and full feature access. Enterprise is custom-priced and unlocks unlimited minutes, custom avatars, SSO, and the full compliance stack.

### Does Synthesia have a free plan?

Yes — but only 3 minutes total, lifetime. The free tier is a sample to evaluate output quality, not a starter for ongoing use.

### Can I create a custom avatar with Synthesia?

Yes, but only on enterprise tiers. The custom avatar process is managed (typically involving an onboarding session and a higher-fidelity capture process), which suits organisations needing strict governance and consent tracking. For self-serve custom avatars at a fixed price, HeyGen at $99/mo is more accessible.

### Is Synthesia GDPR compliant?

Yes — Synthesia is GDPR compliant and also holds SOC 2 Type II and ISO 27001 certifications. Among AI avatar platforms, Synthesia's compliance stack is the most mature, which is the key reason it wins enterprise procurement battles in regulated industries.
