## What Is Midjourney?

Midjourney is an AI image generation platform founded in 2021 by David Holz (co-founder of Leap Motion). Unlike most competitors built on Stable Diffusion derivatives or OpenAI's DALL-E stack, Midjourney runs on its own proprietary diffusion models. The v7 model — current as of the 2026 platform release — is the sixth major model generation and represents years of training refinement focused specifically on aesthetic coherence.

For most of Midjourney's history, the only way to use it was through a Discord bot — users joined the Midjourney server, typed prompts in public channels, and waited for the bot to return image grids. That friction was unusual in an era of polished web UIs, but it also built a distinctive community culture. The 2023 web app launch and the 2025 Pro app updates have closed most of the UX gap, but Discord remains a meaningful part of the workflow for power users.

In 2026, Midjourney is widely considered the image quality leader among mainstream consumer-accessible tools. Adoption is broad across illustration, concept art, editorial design, advertising, fashion visualisation, and independent creative work. The platform has avoided enterprise positioning in favour of serving the prosumer creative market — a strategic choice that shapes pricing, features, and community.

## Key Features

### v7 Model

The v7 model is Midjourney's current default. Based on user reviews across r/Midjourney, the Midjourney Discord, and creator feedback on Twitter/X, v7 represents a meaningful jump in several dimensions — improved hand and anatomy handling (historically a Midjourney weakness), cleaner text rendering (though still behind Ideogram and GPT-4o image generation), better long-prompt coherence, and more consistent stylistic control.

Default output quality on v7 is what users frequently describe as "publishable without edit" for illustration, editorial, and concept work. Photography-style output is strong though occasionally betrays its AI origins under close inspection — skin texture and background consistency remain the weakest areas.

### Style Reference (--sref) and Character Reference (--cref)

Style Reference lets users attach an image whose visual style should be applied to the generated output. Character Reference attaches an image whose subject (character, person, object) should appear in the generation. Both features are central to Midjourney's 2026 workflow — they enable style consistency across a series of images and character continuity across different scenes, which is what most creators want from the tool.

The feature works across v6 and v7 models. Based on user feedback, the results are strong for style transfer (matching the visual mood of a reference) and improving but still imperfect for character reference (maintaining exact facial features across generations remains difficult).

### Web App and Pro App

The web app at midjourney.com is the primary interface for most new users in 2026. The Pro app (web-based with an iPad-optimised version) includes expanded canvas tools, inpainting and outpainting, variation region selection, and batch management. Discord remains available and still has the fastest generation times for many users during off-peak hours.

### Parameters

Midjourney's prompt parameters — --ar (aspect ratio), --v (model version), --s (stylize), --w (weird), --sref, --cref, --p (personalise), and several others — give users fine control over output without requiring prompt engineering expertise. The learning curve is real — new users frequently generate weak output for their first few days before they understand the parameter system — but the control surface is meaningfully deeper than DALL-E 3's or Adobe Firefly's.

### Personalise

The --p (personalise) parameter, introduced in 2024 and refined in 2025, trains a lightweight personal model on the user's image preferences (rated during an onboarding session). Outputs incorporate the user's aesthetic preferences by default. For creators who return to Midjourney daily, this becomes a genuine style multiplier — less prompt tuning required per image, more consistency across a body of work.

### Inpainting, Outpainting, and Variations

The Pro app supports inpainting (select a region and regenerate), outpainting (extend the canvas), and localised variations (pick a subject and generate variations of just that element). These editing capabilities close one of Midjourney's historical gaps versus Stable Diffusion-based tools — you no longer need to leave Midjourney and finish in another app.

### Community Gallery

Every public generation on Midjourney's paid plans (Basic and above, unless Stealth Mode is enabled) appears in the community gallery. This is a genuine source of prompt inspiration and style research — the gallery is searchable by keyword and aesthetic. For users who want to learn what prompts produce what styles, this is the most educational resource the tool offers.

## What We Didn't Like

- **No free tier.** Users can't test the tool before subscribing. Occasional free trial events exist but aren't reliable access points.
- **Prompt adherence is not best-in-class.** For prompts requiring specific spatial relationships, exact object counts, or technical precision, DALL-E 3 and Imagen 3 follow instructions more literally.

## Midjourney Alternatives Worth Considering

**DALL-E 3 (via ChatGPT):** Stronger prompt adherence, literal instruction following, built into ChatGPT. Weaker on pure aesthetic ceiling but wins for users who describe images in detail and want the output to match.

**Leonardo AI:** Better for commercial production workflows. Custom model training, LoRA support, API access, and a more forgiving free tier. Quality is behind Midjourney on default but closes the gap on fine-tuned custom models.

**Ideogram:** Best-in-class text rendering. If your output needs legible text inside the image (posters, ads, infographics), Ideogram wins.

**Flux (via Freepik, Replicate, or similar):** Strong photorealism and open-model flexibility. A better option for users who need a fine-tunable underlying model with API access.

**Adobe Firefly:** The enterprise-safe choice with commercially-safe training data and integration with Creative Cloud. Lower aesthetic ceiling but fewer IP concerns.

## FAQ

### Is Midjourney still the best AI image generator in 2026?

For visual quality on default settings, yes — Midjourney v7 leads our research-based ranking for illustration, editorial, and concept work. For literal prompt adherence, DALL-E 3 wins. For custom model training and commercial API workflows, Leonardo and Flux win. For text inside images, Ideogram wins. "Best" depends on what you need.

### Does Midjourney have a free plan?

No. Midjourney doesn't currently offer a persistent free tier. Occasional free-trial events happen but aren't reliable. The cheapest paid plan is Basic at $8/month annual.

### Can I use Midjourney images commercially?

Yes, on paid plans. Standard, Pro, and Mega include full commercial usage rights. Basic includes commercial rights with certain conditions (notably that if your company has annual revenue over $1M, you need a Pro plan). Stealth Mode on Pro+ keeps generations private from the community gallery, which matters for client work under NDA.

### What's the difference between Midjourney v6 and v7?

v7 improves on hand and anatomy handling, text rendering, prompt coherence on long prompts, and stylistic consistency when using Style Reference. v6 remains available as a model version and some users prefer its aesthetic for specific styles. Most creators default to v7 for new work.

### Does Midjourney still require Discord?

No. The web app at midjourney.com is now the primary interface. Discord remains available and is preferred by some power users for faster generation during off-peak hours, but most users never touch Discord in 2026.

### Can Midjourney generate video?

No. Midjourney is image-only. For AI video generation, tools like Runway, Pika, Kling, or Sora fit that use case.

### Does Midjourney have an API?

Enterprise API access exists but is not available on consumer plans (Basic, Standard, Pro, Mega). For API-driven workflows, Leonardo, Replicate (running Flux or SDXL), and similar tools are better fits.

### Is Midjourney safe for commercial brand work?

For most commercial work, yes. Standard and above include full commercial rights with Stealth Mode available on Pro+ for client confidentiality. For regulated industries or brands with strict IP policies, Adobe Firefly's commercially-safe training data may be a safer fit — Midjourney's training data provenance has been less transparent than some enterprise buyers require.
