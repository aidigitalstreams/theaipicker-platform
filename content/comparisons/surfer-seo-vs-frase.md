## Head-to-Head Comparison

### Content Optimisation

Both tools score your content against top-ranking pages for your target keyword. Surfer's Content Editor provides more data points — NLP term density, content structure analysis, word count targets, and competitor comparison — presented in a real-time sidebar as you write. The scoring is granular and the recommendations are actionable.

Frase's editor is simpler but functional. The term suggestions and content scoring work well for standard blog content. Where Frase falls short is depth — Surfer surfaces more competitive intelligence alongside the editing experience.

**Winner: Surfer SEO** — deeper analysis, more data points.

### Content Audit

This is where the gap widens. Surfer's Content Audit analyses existing published content and identifies optimisation opportunities — missing terms, content gaps, internal linking suggestions. For sites with hundreds of published articles, this feature alone can justify the subscription by improving existing content rather than always creating new.

Frase doesn't offer a comparable content audit feature. Its focus is on creating new optimised content, not improving existing pages.

**Winner: Surfer SEO** — content audit has no Frase equivalent.

### Keyword Clustering

Surfer's keyword clustering groups related keywords that can be targeted by a single page — preventing cannibalisation and identifying content opportunities. This strategic feature saves hours of manual keyword research.

Frase doesn't include keyword clustering. Users need a separate tool (KeySearch, Ahrefs) for this function.

**Winner: Surfer SEO** — included feature vs. not available.
