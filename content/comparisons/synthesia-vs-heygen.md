### Enterprise Compliance and Security

**Winner: Synthesia.** Decisively.

Synthesia holds SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, and GDPR compliance certifications — the baseline requirements for getting an AI tool through most enterprise security teams. Synthesia also offers content moderation controls and avatar consent tracking that are more mature than competitors.

### Custom Avatars

**Winner: HeyGen** for accessibility, **Synthesia** for governance.

HeyGen offers self-serve custom avatar creation from $99/mo. You upload a video sample, HeyGen trains a dedicated avatar model, and you can drive it from any script. This is a real productivity unlock for solo creators and personal brands wanting a digital twin.

Synthesia's custom avatars are enterprise-only — typically requiring a managed onboarding session, higher-fidelity capture, and an enterprise contract. The trade-off is governance: Synthesia's custom avatar process has stronger consent tracking and usage controls, which matters for organisations needing strict avatar lifecycle management.

For solo creators wanting a custom avatar at a self-serve price today, HeyGen wins. For enterprises wanting brand-exact custom avatars with full governance, Synthesia wins.

### Team Collaboration

**Winner: Synthesia.**

Synthesia is built around teams. Role-based permissions, brand kit management, review and approval workflows, and multi-user editing are all baked into the platform from the foundation. This is video production software for organisations, and it shows.

HeyGen offers team features on the Business tier and above, but the core platform is more clearly designed for solo creators and small teams. For enterprise L&D or marketing teams running structured production processes, Synthesia is the more natural fit.

### PowerPoint Integration

**Winner: Synthesia.**

Synthesia's native PowerPoint import converts existing slide decks directly into avatar-led videos. For corporate L&D and internal comms teams sitting on hundreds of training decks, this is a significant productivity unlock. HeyGen has similar integrations but Synthesia's PowerPoint pipeline is more mature.

### Support and Reliability

**Winner: Synthesia, narrowly.**

Both platforms maintain solid uptime and provide responsive support on higher tiers. Synthesia's enterprise tier includes dedicated support and managed onboarding, which HeyGen doesn't quite match. HeyGen's lower tiers occasionally show render-queue delays during peak demand and slower support response.

For most users, both are reliable. For mission-critical enterprise deployments, Synthesia's support posture is the safer bet.
