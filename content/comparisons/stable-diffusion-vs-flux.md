## Quick Verdict

Stable Diffusion has the larger ecosystem — more custom models, LoRAs, ControlNets, and community tools than any other open-source image generator. Flux produces higher-quality images out of the box, especially for text rendering and photorealism, but with a younger ecosystem and higher hardware requirements.

**Choose Stable Diffusion if:** you want the deepest customisation ecosystem and are willing to invest time in setup and fine-tuning.

**Choose Flux if:** you want the best open-source image quality with less configuration and stronger text rendering.

---

## Stable Diffusion Overview

Stable Diffusion is the open-source image generation model that democratised AI art. Released by Stability AI, it can be run locally on consumer GPUs or accessed through hosted platforms. Its greatest strength is the massive community ecosystem: thousands of custom models, LoRAs (style fine-tunes), ControlNets (structural guidance), and ComfyUI/Automatic1111 workflow tools.

Our full review scores Stable Diffusion **80/100**.

## Flux Overview

Flux is a newer open-source image generation model from Black Forest Labs (founded by former Stability AI researchers). It produces higher-quality images out of the box than Stable Diffusion — particularly strong on text rendering (generating readable text within images), photorealism, and prompt adherence. The trade-off is higher VRAM requirements and a younger ecosystem.

Our full review scores Flux **82/100**.

## Head-to-Head Comparison

### Image Quality (Out of Box)

Flux produces noticeably better images with default settings — sharper details, more coherent compositions, stronger photorealism, and significantly better text rendering. Stable Diffusion can match Flux quality but typically requires custom models, careful prompting, and LoRA fine-tunes.

**Winner: Flux** (superior out-of-box quality)

### Text in Images

This is Flux's standout advantage. Generating readable, correctly spelled text within images has been a weakness of AI image generators for years. Flux handles this significantly better than any Stable Diffusion model, making it ideal for mockups, posters, and social media graphics that include text.

**Winner: Flux** (significant advantage)

### Ecosystem and Customisation

Stable Diffusion's ecosystem is unmatched. Thousands of community-trained models on Civitai, hundreds of LoRAs for every imaginable style, ControlNet for structural guidance, and mature workflow tools (ComfyUI, Automatic1111, Forge). This ecosystem lets users achieve results no single model can produce alone.

Flux's ecosystem is growing but significantly smaller. Custom models and LoRAs are emerging, and ComfyUI supports Flux workflows, but the depth of options is a fraction of Stable Diffusion's.

**Winner: Stable Diffusion** (vastly larger ecosystem)

### Hardware Requirements

Stable Diffusion (especially SDXL) runs on consumer GPUs with 8GB+ VRAM. Some optimised versions run on 6GB. This accessibility is a major advantage.

Flux requires more VRAM — 12GB+ recommended for full quality, with quantised versions running on 8GB at reduced quality. This limits accessibility for users with older or mid-range GPUs.

**Winner: Stable Diffusion** (runs on more hardware)

### Prompt Adherence

Flux follows complex prompts more accurately — it better understands spatial relationships, specific details, and multi-element compositions. Stable Diffusion sometimes requires negative prompting and multiple attempts to get specific compositions right.

**Winner: Flux**

### Speed

Stable Diffusion generates images faster on equivalent hardware, particularly with optimised samplers. Flux's transformer architecture is computationally heavier.

**Winner: Stable Diffusion**

### Commercial Licensing

Both offer open-source access. Stable Diffusion uses a relatively permissive license. Flux offers different license tiers — Flux Schnell is Apache 2.0 (fully open), Flux Dev is non-commercial, and Flux Pro requires a commercial licence.

**Winner: Stable Diffusion** (simpler, more permissive licensing across all versions)

## Who Should Choose Stable Diffusion?

Choose Stable Diffusion if you want maximum customisation and control. The ecosystem of models, LoRAs, and tools lets you achieve any visual style imaginable. If you enjoy the process of configuring and fine-tuning — or need to run on modest hardware — Stable Diffusion is the right foundation.

## Who Should Choose Flux?

Choose Flux if you want the best open-source image quality with less configuration. If text rendering matters (mockups, graphics, social content) or if you want strong results from simple prompts without ecosystem deep-dives, Flux delivers more out of the box.

## Our Recommendation

**Flux (82/100)** for best out-of-box quality and text rendering. **Stable Diffusion (80/100)** for deepest customisation ecosystem and broader hardware compatibility. Many advanced users are integrating both into their ComfyUI workflows — using Flux for photorealism and text, Stable Diffusion + LoRAs for specific styles.

## FAQ

**Is Flux better than Stable Diffusion?**
Flux produces better images out of the box, especially for text and photorealism. Stable Diffusion offers a vastly larger customisation ecosystem. "Better" depends on whether you value quality or flexibility.

**Can I run Flux on 8GB VRAM?**
Quantised versions of Flux run on 8GB VRAM at reduced quality. Full quality requires 12GB+. Stable Diffusion runs better on 8GB hardware.

**Is Stable Diffusion free?**
Yes — Stable Diffusion is open-source and free to use locally. Hosted platforms that run it for you may charge fees.

**Which handles text in images better?**
Flux, by a significant margin. Generating readable text within images is one of Flux's standout advantages.
