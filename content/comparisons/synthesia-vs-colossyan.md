## Synthesia Overview

Synthesia is the enterprise incumbent in AI avatar video, founded in 2017 and based in London. The product serves large organisations producing multilingual internal communications, compliance training, product explainer video, and customer-facing training content. The avatar library (230+) is curated for business use; language coverage extends to 140+ languages; the enterprise posture (security certifications, compliance controls, dedicated support) makes it the default procurement choice at scale.

## Colossyan Overview

Colossyan is an AI avatar video platform, also based in London, founded in 2020. Positioning has narrowed deliberately toward learning and development — the product's distinguishing features (Conversation Mode, branching interactivity, SCORM/xAPI export) are exactly the feature set an L&D designer actually needs. Adoption is concentrated in enterprise L&D, compliance, and training teams.

## Head-to-Head Comparison

### Avatar Library and Variety

**Synthesia wins.** 230+ avatars versus Colossyan's 150+. Synthesia's library is broader across demographics, ages, and styles. For teams that need variety across many content pieces, this matters.

Both support custom avatars on higher tiers — Synthesia's custom avatar production is mature and professionally scoped; Colossyan's is comparable in output quality with a similar enterprise workflow.

### Scenario-Based Learning

**Colossyan wins, meaningfully.** Conversation Mode — multiple avatars appearing and speaking to each other within a single scene — is distinctive in the category and is exactly what scenario-based training content needs. Synthesia can approximate the effect by stitching scenes together, but the workflow is materially slower and the maintainability (editing a scenario after a stakeholder review) is worse.

Branching interactivity is Colossyan's second distinguishing feature — learners answer a question mid-video and are routed to different next scenes. For compliance training and soft-skills scenarios, this turns passive video into active learning assets without requiring a separate platform.

### Language Coverage

**Synthesia wins.** 140+ languages (Synthesia) vs 80+ (Colossyan). For multinational organisations producing content across many markets, Synthesia's coverage is meaningfully broader. Both tools' voice quality is competitive within their supported languages.

### Workflow and Learning Curve

**Close — Colossyan's scene-based editor is better fit for modular content, Synthesia's is more universally accessible.** Colossyan's editor is built around scenes-as-building-blocks, which mirrors how L&D designers think about modular courses. Synthesia's editor is more generally applicable — easier for first-time users producing single-purpose videos.

### LMS Integration (SCORM / xAPI)

**Colossyan wins.** SCORM and xAPI export are primary features on Pro and Enterprise. For L&D teams where the output has to plug into Moodle, Cornerstone, SAP SuccessFactors, Docebo, or similar learning management systems, this is the feature that determines whether the tool is adoptable.

Synthesia supports SCORM via enterprise integrations and third-party workflows, but the native experience is less seamless than Colossyan's.

### Support and Procurement Posture

**Synthesia wins.** As the incumbent at enterprise scale, Synthesia has the more mature procurement posture — SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR-aligned data handling, dedicated customer success on Enterprise plans. For regulated industries (financial services, healthcare, public sector) this is often the deciding factor.

Colossyan has a comparable security posture and is adopted in enterprise accounts, but brand-recognition-wise Synthesia tends to clear procurement reviews faster.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Synthesia better than Colossyan?**
For general-purpose avatar video at enterprise scale, yes. For scenario-based L&D specifically — Conversation Mode, branching, SCORM — Colossyan is a better fit. The correct answer depends on the primary use case.

**Does Colossyan have more avatars than Synthesia?**
No. Synthesia has 230+ stock avatars; Colossyan has 150+. Both support custom avatars on higher tiers.

**Which has better SCORM support?**
Colossyan has native SCORM and xAPI export from Pro tier upward. Synthesia supports SCORM via enterprise integrations, which is a less seamless experience than Colossyan's native export.

**Is Conversation Mode unique to Colossyan?**
Colossyan is the clearest implementation in the avatar video category. Other tools can approximate the effect with scene stitching, but Colossyan's single-scene multi-avatar execution is distinctive.

**Which is cheaper for L&D use cases?**
Colossyan Pro at $49/month (annual) includes SCORM and Conversation Mode — the two L&D-critical features — at a lower price than Synthesia Creator ($67/month). For the specific L&D use case, Colossyan wins on value.
