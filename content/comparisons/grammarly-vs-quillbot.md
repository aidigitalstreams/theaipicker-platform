## Head-to-Head Comparison

### Grammar & Error Checking

Grammarly leads here by a significant margin. Its grammar engine catches errors with 93-98% accuracy across over 400 grammar rules, including nuanced issues like misplaced modifiers, subject-verb agreement in complex sentences, and comma splices. The suggestions include explanations of why something is wrong, which helps users learn.

QuillBot includes a grammar checker, but it functions as a secondary feature rather than a core strength. The error detection is competent but not as thorough or accurate as Grammarly's purpose-built engine.

**Winner: Grammarly** — grammar checking is its core identity and it shows.

### Paraphrasing & Rewriting

QuillBot is purpose-built for this and it shows. Ten rewriting modes give precise control over how text is restructured. Academic mode maintains formal register. Creative mode produces genuine variation. Fluency mode smooths awkward phrasing. The ability to adjust a "Synonym" slider controls how aggressively the tool rewrites — from minimal changes to complete restructuring.

Grammarly's rewriting capabilities exist through GrammarlyGO, but they're more limited. You can ask GrammarlyGO to rewrite a paragraph or adjust tone, but the fine-grained mode control that QuillBot offers doesn't exist.

**Winner: QuillBot** — 10 specialised modes versus Grammarly's general rewriting.

### AI Writing Assistance

Grammarly's GrammarlyGO can draft content from prompts, extend existing text, and adjust tone (professional, friendly, confident, etc.). It's not a full content generator like Jasper or Copy.ai, but it handles quick drafting tasks within the editing workflow well.

QuillBot doesn't offer AI content generation. Its role is transforming existing text, not creating new text from scratch. If you need to generate content, QuillBot isn't the tool.

**Winner: Grammarly** — GrammarlyGO adds content generation; QuillBot only transforms existing text.

## FAQ

**Can I use Grammarly and QuillBot together?**
Yes. Grammarly's browser extension and QuillBot's web app work alongside each other. Use QuillBot for paraphrasing, paste the result into your document, and let Grammarly catch any remaining errors.

**Is QuillBot good for academic writing?**
Yes — the Academic mode maintains formal register and avoids colloquialisms. The citation generator adds APA, MLA, and Chicago-style citations. For students and researchers, QuillBot is a strong tool.

**Does Grammarly work in Google Docs?**
Yes. The browser extension integrates with Google Docs, showing suggestions inline as you type. It also works in Microsoft Word (via add-in), Gmail, LinkedIn, and most web-based text fields.
